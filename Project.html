Computer Science Principles

Block Parkour

September 2018

In my Computer Science Principles class, my partner, Edmund Feng, and I created a mini-game called "Block Parkour" in Scratch. Over a few weeks we had to create a game or story which had user interaction and multiple levels. We brainstormed many ideas and we chose to make a parkour game with the player controlling a block using arrow keys. The player had to get the block through obstacles, mainly jumping from level to level. We also added lava in some levels which the block could not touch. My partner and I kept thorough documentation for referring back to the earlier stages of the project. We had multiple difficulties such as programming the lava in some levels, but we worked together and figured it out. One more difficulty was finding the right gravity for the block for all the levels, but through much trial and error we found the right gravity number. I learned many new functions in this project and was able to understand them easily through the help of my peers. All in all, it was a great learning experience for me and I got to make some new friends as well.

Mechanical Winch System

October 2019

In my class Honors Principles Of Engineering, my partners, Shaan M., Romyr C., Sai K., and I created a mechanical winch for our 1.2.5 engineering project. The purpose of this project was to test our teamwork skills, creativity, and our understanding of the partners given to us to build the prototype. The background of the project was that a construction company needed us to build a scale prototype of a winch for them to lift heavy objects. My partners and I kept thorough documentation of our project and have fully stated to constraints and the rules of this project. In this project I mainly contributed to the engineering aspect, building the actual winch and creatively thinking what was the most efficiently ay for this project to complete its given task. With the help of Romyr, he and I built the winch, my specific contribution being the building of the structure of the winch, making it a compact and efficient design. I also did a large part of the documentation such as the design brief and parts of the modifications table. Some problems we had while building this winch was how to implement the motor to the structure of the winch. Our given parts were not all from the same brand so implementing them both together was a challenge, but through some creativity and teamwork, we were able to successfully mesh the motor to the other systems of the gears. Overall this project went extremely well and the objective of the project was fulfilled.

Aerodynamic Design

January 2020

In my Honors Principles Of Engineering class, my partners, Kaylen Carter-Chisolm, Sai Kolla, Isha Nayak, and I created a rocket design as a proposal for toy rockets. We had to create two design's each and then test them for distance, style, and altitude. We then had to pick the best design and modify it one last time and propose that design to the clients. We kept thorough documentation for all of our designs and our final design. We explained our designs as well. While building the rockets we had limited supplies so it was a challenge building a strong rocket. The supplied we had were paper and tape and thats quite it. During the construction there were many issues we had to face like the flexibility of the paper and the way we had to layer it. It was difficult to layer the paper but with some teamwork we were able to help each other out with their induvidual designs. I created the winning design with an altitude of 60 ft and a distance of 90 ft. I was also the launcher for our group as well. My partner's and I were able to communicate efficiently to produce good designs and we were able complete our documentation proffesionally to display all of our potential designs.
