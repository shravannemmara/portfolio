During my time in the Engineering Academy program, I have met several industry professionals through workplace field trips and guest speakers. These guest speakers and trips were given for us to explore different areas of engineering such as chemical engineering and civil engineering. Through this program, I was exposed to many different fields and they have been very influential in my career process.

One guest speaker that was very noteworthy for me was Ms. Emma Schoenthol, Civil engineer at Bellecci & Associates Inc. The majority of Ms. Schoenthal's work is civilian projects such as downtown projects and sidewalk reconstruction. She designs blueprints and ideas for clients, mainly the city managers. She also writes reports for completed projects as well. During this presentation she showed us what the majority of her work is and also showed us plans for a new project that they are working on. We were able to learn new engineering terms and lingo in the Civil Engineering field. One phrase that stuck with me was the term "pork chop" island. It is the small island in an intersection to get to the main stoplight. This presentation was very influential for me because Civil Engineering is my main career path and that is what I want to be when I grow up. Though I believe I will do better in Residential Engineering I still think that this presentation was very informative. One key takeaway from this presentation is that the maximum slope for a sidewalk for ADA accessibility is 7.4%

Along with guest speakers coming to DHS for the Engineering Academy, the students, including myself, get to go on many field trips and see facilities that take part in engineering fields. One memorable field trip we recently went on was to Roche Molecular Labs in Pleasonton, California. In this field trip we were able to see the inner workings of America's leading corporation in Molecular Diagnostics. We were able to see the labs where they cultured bacteria samples. We were also given a tour of their mobile sales bus, used to travel the country to exhibit their technology to potential clients. This field trip was influential for me because it made me really think about a career in Biotechnology and this field was one of my options before Civil Engineering as well. Their amazing internship program also tempted me to join because there are many oppurtunities in this field to grow and grasp knowledge. One part of the field trip that I especially liked was the mentor question part. We were able to ask questions to employees there from different backgrounds and see how they worked and what they did for a job. This part of the trip really stood out to me because I was able to gain so many new perspectives on my career and that pathway. I learned that a career isn't set-in-stone and that I could change whenever I wanted and that was something I genuinely needed to hear. One key takeaway from this trip is that there are many types of jobs in this company such as sales, doctors, and lab work so there are many options to choose from while still staying in the Engineering field.
